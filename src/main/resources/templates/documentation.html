Frequently asked questions

What is a "mock"?

Mocking something can mean a bunch of different things. However, within Mega Mock, when we refer to "mocks" we mean a fake API endpoint. Whenever you work on a new microservice or a proof of concept, you often have to rely upon third-party APIs and integrations.

Because of how things work in software development, it is not always possible to immediately use a third-party endpoint. Maybe your team is still waiting for someone else to finish their work. Or it could be that your manager forgot to request a new API key for your team.

In any case, your team is blocked. This is where Mega Mock can help you out. Instead of waiting around hoping someone will unblock your team, simply fire up Mega Mock and create the endpoints you need yourself!

Obviously the data returned from the endpoints will be fake, but it will at least allow your team to keep moving without having to wait for others to do their jobs. This is what we mean with mocking: you fake an API's response yourself.

Can I use Mega Mock for integration tests?

Absolutely! Mega Mock is actually super useful for integration tests. It eliminates the need to rely upon external parties when testing your services.

What about testing unreliable connections? Does Mega Mock allow us to mock failures?

Sure does! Mega Mock includes a so-called "Team Rocket" feature. Enabling this functionality will cause Mega Mock to mess with your connections by randomly disconnecting or delaying your responses. All this behaviour can be configured to fit your project's needs.
